Being born in Addis Abeba, Ethiopia, and coming to the United States was frightening as a 5 year old. Being a first-generation immigrant student has shaped how I view education, opportunity, and responsibility. I still think about being that little girl standing on stage as valedictorian during my 5th and 8th grade promotions, not fully understanding what the future would hold. From those moments to graduating in the top 5% of my high school class, it’s been a full-circle experience.

Pursuing higher education isn’t a sole achievement but instead one that I share with many.

Shout out to the amazing people I met in extracurricular activities: SGA, NSBE, YPP, EASA, the Montgomery Parks team and MIT’s Introduction to Technology, Engineering, and Science. These were places were I gained eye-opening experiences and participated in conversations that expanded my understanding.

I’m beyond excited to share that I’ll be attending Yale University as part of the Class of 2030 with the prospective major in Ethics, Politics and Economics on the Pre-Law Track. I’m also grateful to have received a Montgomery County Alliance of Black Educators Scholarship and a Ruth & Norman Rales-Patricia Baier O’Neill Scholarship Recognizing Academic Excellence, which helped make this milestone possible.

Looking forward to beginning this next chapter in New Haven.

I told an AI to play Flappy Bird. 50,000 deaths later — it's unstoppable. 👇
Most people think AI is magic.
It's not.
It's failure. Repeated. At scale.
Here's exactly what 50,000 episodes looks like:
🔴 Episode 1 → dead in 0.2 seconds
 🔴 Episode 10,000 → still dying instantly
 🔴 Episode 30,000 → stuck. every reward = -0.89. no progress.
 🟡 Episode 45,000 → something clicks. first 5 pipes cleared.
 🟢 Episode 50,000 → 17 pipes. consecutive. no help.
The AI was never told what to do.
It figured it out entirely on its own.
The mistakes that nearly killed this project:
❌ Learning rate too high → unlearning faster than learning
 ❌ Target network syncing every 10 steps → complete instability
 ❌ Replay buffer too small → AI forgot everything overnight
 ❌ One hidden layer → hard ceiling at 3 pipes, couldn't break through
Fixed every single one. Rebuilt from scratch. Ran it again.
That's engineering.
What this actually is under the hood:
"Built using PyTorch, debugged extensively, with the core DQN logic written and iterated on by me."
→ Custom Experience Replay Buffer
 → Policy Network + Target Network
 → Epsilon-greedy Exploration
 → Trained on GPU (RTX 4050, CUDA)
Still training.
17 pipes is not the ceiling. 👀
